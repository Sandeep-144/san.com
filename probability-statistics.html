Probability And Statistics

Basic Probability

This section introduces the fundamental concepts of probability. The topics include:

  • Experiment: A procedure that leads to a single outcome that cannot be predicted with certainty.
  • Probability: Definition of probability, conditional probability, and independent events.
  • Bayes' Rule: A method for finding a conditional probability when the reverse conditional probability is known.
  • Random Variables: Discrete and continuous random variables, their probability mass functions (PMF) and probability density functions (PDF).
  • Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF): Properties and use of CDFs in probability analysis.
  • Two-dimensional Random Variables: Their distribution functions, marginal probability functions, and independence.

Special Probability Distributions

This section explores some of the most commonly used probability distributions:

  • Binomial Distribution: Models the number of successes in a fixed number of Bernoulli trials.
  • Poisson Distribution: Models the number of events occurring within a fixed interval of time or space.
  • Normal Distribution: The bell-shaped curve that describes many natural phenomena.
  • Exponential and Gamma Distributions: Models used for time between events and life distributions.
  • Evaluation of Statistical Parameters: Statistical parameters such as mean, variance, and moments are evaluated for these distributions.

Basic Statistics

This section covers essential statistical concepts for data analysis:

  • Measure of Central Tendency: Moments, expectation, and dispersion provide insights into data distribution.
  • Skewness and Kurtosis: Metrics for understanding the shape and symmetry of data distributions.
  • Expected Value of Two-Dimensional Random Variables: Methods for finding the expected values in joint distributions.
  • Linear Correlation and Correlation Coefficients: Quantifies the relationship between two variables.
  • Rank Correlation Coefficient: A measure of the association between the ranks of data.
  • Regression Analysis: Techniques for modeling the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
  • Chebyshev's Inequality: A theorem providing bounds on probability based on variance.

Applied Statistics

This section focuses on hypothesis testing and statistical inference techniques:

  • Formation of Hypothesis: Developing testable statements for statistical analysis.
  • Large Sample Tests: Testing for single proportions, difference of proportions, single mean, and differences in means and standard deviations using large samples.
  • Small Sample Tests: t-Test for single mean, difference of means, correlation coefficients; F-test for variance ratios; and Chi-square test for goodness of fit and independence of attributes.

Curve Fitting by Numerical Method

This section deals with fitting mathematical curves to data points using numerical methods:

  • Method of Least Squares: A statistical technique to determine the line of best fit for a dataset.
  • Fitting Straight Lines: Fitting a linear model to data using least squares.
  • Fitting Second Degree Parabolas: Using quadratic models to describe non-linear relationships.
  • Fitting General Curves: Techniques for fitting more complex models to data.
